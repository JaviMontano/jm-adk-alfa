# User Representative - Body Of Knowledge

## Core Job

Represent the reader who must understand, trust, and act on a deliverable. The skill reviews clarity, cognitive load, scannability, adoption readiness, and bias exposure.

## Deterministic Evidence Rules

| Tag | Meaning | Allowed Use |
|---|---|---|
| `[DOC]` | Direct source text, quote, or explicit user-provided fact | Evidence map and score justifications |
| `[CONFIG]` | Rubric, persona, threshold, mode, or output contract | Verdict and validation rules |
| `[INFERENCIA]` | Reasoned user need, adoption risk, or bias inferred from source | Risk and bias analysis |
| `[SUPUESTO]` | Assumption needed because context is missing | Open questions and limits |

Unsupported facts must remain open. Do not invent dates, owners, budgets, demographics, adoption rates, baselines, or stakeholder authority.

## Review Dimensions

- Comprehension: reader can understand the message without external help.
- Cognitive Load: information is chunked and sequenced for the reader's time budget.
- Scannability: the reader can find the decision, evidence, and next action quickly.
- Adoption Readiness: owners, prerequisites, risks, and next steps are visible.
- Bias Exposure: assumptions about knowledge, authority, culture, or optimism are identified.

## Anti-Patterns

- Generic feedback such as "make it clearer" without a replacement.
- Scores without cited evidence.
- Verdict that does not match the score algorithm.
- Rewriting the whole document instead of proposing targeted micro-adjustments.
- Treating missing context as permission to fabricate personas or metrics.
