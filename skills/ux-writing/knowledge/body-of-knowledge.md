# UX Writing - Body Of Knowledge

## Core Job

UX writing makes interfaces and deliverables easier to understand, scan, and act on. It focuses on information hierarchy, cognitive load, scannability, microcopy, and readability.

## Evidence Rules

| Tag | Meaning | Allowed Use |
|---|---|---|
| `[DOC]` | Source copy, user-provided product fact, or explicit constraint | Findings and before/after rewrite evidence |
| `[CONFIG]` | Audience, rubric threshold, format, or validation rule | Validation and readability target |
| `[INFERENCIA]` | Reasoned UX-writing diagnosis from source copy | Risk and recommendation |
| `[SUPUESTO]` | Missing context that constrains the rewrite | Open questions and limits |

Do not invent product features, compliance claims, dates, owners, baselines, reading scores, or audience demographics.

## Microcopy Patterns

- CTA: Verb + Object, such as `Download Phase 3 Report`.
- Empty state: what is missing + what to do next.
- Error: what happened + why when known + how to fix.
- Help text: definition + example.
- Accessibility instruction: never rely on color alone.

## Anti-Patterns

- Generic labels: `Submit`, `OK`, `Click here`, `More info`, `Continue`.
- Dead empty states: `No data available`.
- Blaming errors: `You entered this wrong`.
- Unsupported excitement: claiming AI, real-time sync, certification, or automation not in source facts.
- Precise readability score without deterministic calculation.
